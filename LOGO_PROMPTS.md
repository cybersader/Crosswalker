# Crosswalker Logo Prompts

## What NOT to do

- No generic "nodes and lines" graphs — looks like every other tech logo
- No grid/circuit board patterns — no identity
- No abstract blobs — unmemorable
- The logo needs a **distinctive silhouette** recognizable at 16px

## Brand DNA

**What Crosswalker does**: Bridges between structured worlds (frameworks ↔ evidence ↔ knowledge). The "cross" in crosswalk is literal — paths crossing, worlds connecting.

**The feeling**: Authoritative but accessible. Cybersecurity-professional, not startup-cute. Think: a tool that compliance officers trust.

| Hex | Use |

## Base instruction (prepend to all)

```
Minimal flat vector logo mark. Clean geometric shapes. No photorealism. Must have a distinctive silhouette recognizable at 16px. Software product icon quality (Linear, Raycast, Arc level). No text unless specified.
```

## Concept A: The Bridge

Two distinct shapes (representing different frameworks/systems) connected by a bold crossing bridge element. The bridge IS the logo — it's the crosswalk itself.

```
Two small squares side by side, slightly offset vertically, connected by two bold diagonal lines that cross each other between them forming an X bridge. The squares represent different systems; the crossing lines represent the crosswalk mapping. Teal (#00d4aa) on dark (#0d1117). The X crossing point is the visual center and focal point. Thick, confident strokes. The overall silhouette reads as a bowtie or hourglass shape.
```

## Concept B: The Compass Cross

A compass-rose or crosshair shape where four paths converge at a center point — representing multiple frameworks all meeting through Crosswalker.

```
A bold plus/cross shape where each of the four arms is a different length, representing different framework hierarchies converging at a center point. The center point is a small filled circle (the crosswalk nexus). Each arm ends in a small square or node. The arms have subtle notches or tick marks like a ruler or hierarchy depth indicator. Teal (#00d4aa) on dark (#0d1117). The silhouette is an asymmetric cross — distinct from a medical cross or Red Cross. Reads as "convergence point."
```

## Concept C: The Woven Knot

Two ribbon-like paths weaving over and under each other, forming a knot or Celtic-cross-like interlock. Represents frameworks intertwined through crosswalking.

```
Two thick ribbon paths, one horizontal and one vertical, woven together in an over-under pattern forming a simple square knot or interlocking cross at the center. Where one ribbon goes over the other, there's a subtle shadow/gap showing the weave. The ribbons extend slightly beyond the knot. Teal (#00d4aa) ribbons on dark (#0d1117). The weave/interlock is the key visual — it shows two separate things bound together. Bold, geometric, not ornate.
```

## Concept D: The Gateway

A doorway or portal shape — an arch or opening — with connections flowing through it. Crosswalker as the gateway between unstructured chaos and organized knowledge.

```
A bold geometric arch or doorway shape (like a rounded rectangle open at the bottom, or a U rotated 180°). Inside the arch, 3 small dots connected by lines — a mini knowledge graph emerging through the gateway. Outside the arch (above), scattered small dots — unorganized data. The arch itself is the Crosswalker identity. Teal (#00d4aa) arch, lighter teal dots, dark (#0d1117) background. The silhouette reads as a portal or bridge opening.
```

## Concept E: The CW Ligature

The letters C and W sharing a stroke — a typographic ligature where the right side of C flows into the left side of W, forming a single connected glyph.

```
A custom typographic ligature of the letters C and W where they share a common vertical stroke in the middle. The C's opening faces right, the W rises from the shared stroke. The shared stroke represents the bridge/crosswalk between the two. Bold geometric weight, like a mono or display font. Teal (#00d4aa) on dark (#0d1117). Not decorative — clean, engineered, like a technical monogram. The shared stroke is slightly thicker or glows subtly to emphasize the connection.
```

## Variant modifiers (append to any concept)

| Variant | Append this |
|---------|-------------|
| Light bg | Change to: dark teal (#0a6b56) on white (#f8f9fa). No glow. |
| Mono | Single color (#0d1117) on transparent. Simplify to minimum strokes. |
| Favicon | Simplify aggressively. Must read at 16×16. Drop any detail that disappears at small size. |
| + Wordmark | Below the mark: "CROSSWALKER" in Inter or Geist font, 600 weight, 0.12em spacing, white. |
| Multi-color | Add a second color: one element in red (#e06c75), one in green (#98c379) alongside teal. Three systems meeting. |
